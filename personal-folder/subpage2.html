The first 4D platonic solid is the 5-cell, 5 tetrahedra joined 3 to a vertex.

The second 4D platonic solid is the 8-cell, 8 cubes joined 3 to a vertex.

The third is the 16-cell, 16 tetrahedra joined 4 to a vertex.

The fourth is the 24-cell, 24 octahedra joined 3 to a vertex. This is the only one in 4D that doesn't have a 3D counterpart.

The fifth is the 120-cell, 120 dodecahedra joined 3 to a vertex.

The sixth and final is the 600-cell, 600 tetrahedra joined 5 to a vertex.
