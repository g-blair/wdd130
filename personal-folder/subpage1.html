The first platonic solid is the tetrahedron, 4 triangles joined 3 to a vertex.

The second platonic solid is the cube, 6 squares joined 3 to a vertex.

The third is the octahedron, 8 triangles joined 4 to a vertex.

The fourth is the dodecahedron, 12 pentagons joined 3 to a vertex.

The fifth and final is the icosahedron, 20 triangles joined 5 to a vertex.
